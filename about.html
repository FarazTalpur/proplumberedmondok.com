Edmond's Historical Plumbing Challenges

Edmond, initially a water stop for the Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe Railway, began its development as a city in 1887. Its early plumbing systems reflected the technological limitations of the era, using materials like cast iron and galvanized steel. While these methods served well in their time, modern advances demand upgrades for efficiency and compliance with current plumbing codes.
