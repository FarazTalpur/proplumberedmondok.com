Frequently Asked Questions

Leak Detection and Repair (LDAR) is a systematic process used to identify and repair leaks in industrial facilities, aiming to prevent the release of harmful emissions, often required by environmental regulations.

LDAR is crucial for minimizing environmental pollution, improving safety by reducing the risk of hazardous leaks, ensuring regulatory compliance, and maintaining efficient operation by preventing product loss.

Common methods for LDAR include optical gas imaging, ultrasonic leak detectors, and gas sniffers which help detect the presence and quantify the emission levels of leaks.

The frequency of leak detection depends on regulatory requirements and industrial best practices, but it typically involves regular inspections ranging from monthly to annual assessments.

Failure to implement LDAR can lead to environmental damage, legal penalties, safety hazards, loss of product, and reputational damage for companies involved in the release of harmful emissions.
