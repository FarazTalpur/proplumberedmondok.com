Frequently Asked Questions

Signs of a burst pipe include a sudden drop in water pressure, water discoloration, unusual noises like hissing, visible water damage on walls or ceilings, and an unexpected increase in your water bill.

First, turn off the main water supply to prevent further flooding. Then, switch off the electricity in affected areas to avoid electrical hazards. After ensuring safety, document damage for insurance purposes and call a professional plumber for repairs.

While minor leaks and repairs might be addressed with some DIY solutions, a burst pipe typically requires professional intervention due to the complexity and risks involved. It's best to consult with a licensed plumber for a thorough and safe repair.

To prevent burst pipes, insulate pipes in unheated areas, keep a consistent indoor temperature, let faucets trickle during freezing weather, and regularly check for leaks or corrosion. Proper maintenance and timely upgrades are crucial for preventing pipe bursts.

The cost to repair a burst pipe can vary widely depending on factors like location, accessibility, and the extent of damage. On average, homeowners might expect to pay between $200 and $600, but complex scenarios could cost more.
