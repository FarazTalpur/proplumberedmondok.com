Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow testing is the process of checking a plumbing system to ensure that the water flow is only traveling in one direction, preventing contaminants from entering the potable water supply.

Backflow testing is crucial for preventing contamination of drinking water from backflow, which can occur due to changes in water pressure. This testing helps maintain safe and clean water for consumption.

Most local regulations require backflow prevention devices to be tested annually by a certified professional to ensure they are functioning correctly and to maintain compliance with health and safety standards.

Backflow testing must be conducted by a certified backflow tester or a licensed plumber with the proper training and certification to perform these tests and maintain your compliance with local regulations.

If a backflow test fails, the backflow prevention device is not working properly, and immediate repairs or replacements are necessary to protect the water supply from contamination and to comply with local health regulations.
